A New Path Provided

from student's perspectives

Year Up Puget Sound provides a gateway for young adults into the careers and lives they want. This rings true for even the current generation of students within the program (as of 11/6/2020). As student’s in the Software Development & Testing track, each one of us comes from a unique background, but culminate together for the sake of education and opportunity.

Year Up has provided many of us with a with a chance to receive higher education that may not have been available before. It has also given us new insight to people of different cultures and life experiences. Giving us a new sense of community among classmates.



“It has been way more than a college program to me because there is a whole lot more community that I have seen in actual college experience.”
– Alejandro Chavez (Year Up Student, SD&T Class 20)


“Without my classmates and the support from Year Up staff, I would be lost in the stresses of the world today with the [Covid-19] pandemic. I certainly would be a lot farther away from the life and career I want than I am now.”
-Maria O’Sullivan (Year Up Student, SD&T Class 20)


From Past to Present

Gerald Chertavian, technology entrepreneur and Wall Street banker founded Year Up in 2000. Inspired by his many years as a Big Brother, he believed young people of any background could accomplish anything. 20 years later and 29,000+ students served, Chertavain’s dream continues to inspire students.

Year Up is committed to evaluating and improving its program. It’s focus on measurable, scientific improvement helps provide the most efficient services for student’s and it’s partners.

    According to Year Up's offical website:
  • 90% Of grads employed and/or enrolled in postsecondary education within 4 months of graduation
  • Year Up interns have a 90% satifiaction rating from managers
  • $42,000 is the average starting annual salary of Year Up grads
  • Year Up graduates have a 53% increase in wage gains 6-9 months after graduation
Learn more in the official "Pathways for Advancing Careers and Education (PACE) Evaluation" report
